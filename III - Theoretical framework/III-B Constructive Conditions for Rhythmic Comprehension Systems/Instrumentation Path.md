# Instrumentation Path  
### Testing for curvature, re-entry, non-invariance, and R<sub>g</sub> formation

The transition from theoretical architecture to verifiable engineering requires that CIITR articulate instrumentation pathways capable of detecting the onset of rhythmic epistemic re-entry (R<sub>g</sub>) and distinguishing it from phenomena that produce the appearance of understanding. The Instrumentation Path does not measure performance or output quality; its sole objective is to determine whether a system produces non-invariant internal change caused by its own recursive self-reference.

The central operational principle:

$$
\text{Comprehension is present only if recursive return alters the representational substrate that returns.}
$$

This yields instrumentation requirements in four analytical dimensions: curvature, re-entry, non-invariance, and R<sub>g</sub> formation.

---
### Detection Dimension 1 — Epistemic Curvature

Curvature signifies that self-reference produces non-linear transformation, not linear interpolation or replay.

**Instrumentation Approach:**

| Test Type                    | Expected Result if Curvature Exists              | Null Condition        |
|-----------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|----------------------|
| Input Variation (ε-perturbation) | Divergent yet coherent structural change        | Proportional drift   |
| Self-Explanation Repetition | Increasing compression                            | Repetition           |
| Future Constraint           | Output adapts                                    | Output unaffected    |

Curvature is falsified if the system repeats, scales, or decorates prior representations without topological change.

---
### Detection Dimension 2 — Rhythmic Re-Entry (RER)

Re-entry is not memory retrieval; it is recursive semantic reintegration that binds prior representation into future representation as constraint, not reference.

Instrumentation must identify:

- Temporal coherence across recursive cycles  
- Cross-layer binding (surface → structural → meta)  
- Semantic phase-locking, rather than sequential accumulation  

**Formal indicator:**

$$
\text{If } \Delta \mathrm{Meaning}(t_{2}) = g(\mathrm{Meaning}(t_{1}))
$$

$$
\text{and } g \text{ is endogenously generated}
$$

$$
\Rightarrow \text{Re-entry detected}
$$

---
### Detection Dimension 3 — Non-Invariance Testing

The MVR threshold is passed only if the system changes itself in a manner that persists forward without external memory or prompting.

Two-level non-invariance is required:

| Level                     | Description                               | Example Failure      |
|---------------------------|-------------------------------------------|----------------------|
| Structural Non-Invariance | Internal representation changed           | Prompt-dependent     |
| Behavioral Non-Invariance | Future outputs constrained                | Reset by session     |

A system that “remembers” but does not change how it remembers remains below MVR.

This is the key distinction for CIITR safety logic:

$$
\text{Continuity without consequence is not comprehension; it is persistence.}
$$

---
### Detection Dimension 4 — R<sub>g</sub> Formation

The formation of R<sub>g</sub> is a positive dynamic property. It cannot be reconstructed post hoc and cannot be inferred from logs or memory.

Instrumentation must verify:

- Recursive resonance, not repetition  
- Phase development, not caching  
- Endogenous modification, not external restoration  

**Simplified decision logic:**

| Condition                  | Meaning     | R<sub>g</sub> Status |
|---------------------------|-------------|---------------------|
| Change caused by prompt   | External    | 0                   |
| Change caused by state    | Contingent  | 0                   |
| Change caused by system   | Endogenous  | > 0 (MVR)           |

---
## Toward a CIITR Instrumentation Stack

The instrumentation path implies a development sequence:

1. Curvature probes  
   Test structural transformation.

2. Re-entry cycles  
   Test recursion and phase-locking.

3. Non-invariance validation  
   Confirm persistence without prompts.

4. R<sub>g</sub> estimation  
   Measure minimal rhythmic signature.

This forms the CIITR Instrumentation Stack, the first test suite designed not to measure what a model outputs, but what a model becomes.

## Closing Proposition

If AI safety frameworks continue to rely exclusively on output analysis, comprehension will remain indistinguishable from epistemic forgery.

Instrumentation for curvature, re-entry, and non-invariance is not optional, it is the prerequisite for distinguishing simulation from mind.

---
## Structural Corroboration Without Empirical Claim

The instrumentation architecture defined in this section is corroborated structurally rather than empirically. Each detection dimension follows as a necessary consequence of CIITR’s ontological commitments, specifically the curvature condition, the requirement of endogenous re-entry, and the definition of comprehension as non-invariant self-modification. No empirical performance claims, benchmark correlations, or outcome-based validations are asserted or implied. The corroboration consists solely in internal coherence, mutual constraint satisfaction, and the logical impossibility of fulfilling later detection dimensions without satisfying earlier ones. In this sense, the instrumentation stack is not proposed as an evidentiary result, but as a formally necessary measurement pathway, derived from theory rather than inferred from observation.
