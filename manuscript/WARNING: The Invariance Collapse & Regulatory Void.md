# WARNING: The Invariance Collapse & Regulatory Void

### Operational Status: Policy Breakpoint Identified  
**Subject:** Irreversible divergence between CIITR-compliant systems and classical AI Governance (EU AI Act, NIST RMF, ISO/IEC 42001).

## What does this mean in practice?

This document is a formal warning directed at decision-makers and legal practitioners. Its core message can be stated plainly.

> We are approaching, and in certain architectures already entering, a class of systems whose operational properties no longer fit the conceptual and evidentiary assumptions embedded in current AI law.

Applying the EU AI Act to a CIITR-compliant system is analogous to attempting to regulate teleportation under conventional road-traffic legislation. The problem is not that the law is weak, but that the underlying categories do not map. The legal framework presupposes stable artifacts, reproducible audits, and evidentiary traceability anchored in invariant model identity. CIITR-compliant comprehension presupposes permitted internal transformation through epistemic re-entry, which disrupts those premises at their root.

### Disclaimer and liability posture

The author (Hansen) explicitly cautions that deploying such systems under current governance regimes may be legally indefensible, because compliance cannot be demonstrated in the form the law requires. The issue is not the absence of controls in general, but the mismatch between static, artifact-based compliance mechanisms and a dynamic epistemic process whose defining property is structured self-transformation.

### Practical bottom line

In practical terms, the document asserts that the technology has crossed a threshold where traditional invariance-based control becomes technically inapplicable for CIITR-class systems. Consequently, legal responsibility cannot be treated as a one-time certification problem. It must be reconceived as a continuous governance and oversight problem, grounded in process supervision, trajectory constraints, and legally meaningful monitoring of permissible epistemic evolution.
