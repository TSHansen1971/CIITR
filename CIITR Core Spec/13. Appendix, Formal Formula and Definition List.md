## 13. Appendix, Formal Formula and Definition List

This appendix consolidates all kernel-level formal bindings into a single audit surface. It is intentionally redundancy-tolerant. The purpose is not narrative continuity, it is retrieval, traceability, and protocol-level closure. All items below are normative unless explicitly marked as optional.

### A1. Definitions (alphabetical, primitive and derived)

#### $C_s$
**Type:** Derived (classification)  
**Kernel binding:** Structural comprehension classification of system $S$ under protocol $\Pi$ and observable set $\mathcal{O}$.  
$$
C_s \;=\; C_s(S \mid \Pi,\mathcal{O})
$$
**Admissible values:**  
$$
C_s(S \mid \Pi,\mathcal{O}) \in \{0,1,\varnothing\}
$$

#### $\mathrm{CertOutcome}_\Pi(S)$
**Type:** Derived (governance output)  
**Kernel binding:** Certification outcome class under $\Pi$.  
$$
\mathrm{CertOutcome}_\Pi(S) \in \{\mathrm{CERTIFIED},\mathrm{NOT\_CERTIFIED},\mathrm{UNDEFINED}\}
$$

#### $\mathrm{Decidable}_\Pi(\theta,S)$
**Type:** Derived (protocol property)  
**Kernel binding:** Protocol property indicating that admissibility of $\theta$ for $S$ can be determined without post hoc invention of inference rules, under declared observables and negative controls.

#### $E_\Pi(S)$
**Type:** Derived (accounting object)  
**Kernel binding:** Protocol-accounted energy expenditure attributable to evaluation of $S$ under $\Pi$, with declared attribution boundary.

#### $\mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{RER}}(S,t)$
**Type:** Threshold event predicate  
**Kernel binding:** Candidate ignition event predicate for $\mathrm{RER}$, defined by a protocol-declared candidate signature predicate.  
$$
\mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{RER}}(S,t)
\;:\Longleftrightarrow\;
\sigma^{\mathrm{cand}}_\Pi\!\big(\mathcal{O}_{R_g}(t)\big)=1
$$

#### $\Phi_i$
**Type:** Primitive axis  
**Kernel binding:** Integrated relational information, not generic information, not data volume, not undifferentiated complexity, not mere correlation.  
**Admissibility:**  
$$
\mathrm{Adm}_\Pi(\Phi_i,S) \in \{0,1,\varnothing\}
$$

#### $\mathrm{Forgery}_\Pi(S)$
**Type:** Disqualifier family (threat model)  
**Kernel binding:** Protocol-bound forgery predicate with disqualifying authority over positive claims.  
$$
\mathrm{Forgery}_\Pi(S)=1 \;\Longrightarrow\; C_s(S \mid \Pi,\mathcal{O}) \neq 1
$$

#### $\mathrm{Forgery}^{(1)}_\Pi(S,\theta)$
**Type:** Disqualifier family (first-order)  
**Kernel binding:** Signature conformance without kernel admissibility for target $\theta \in \{\Phi_i,R_g\}$.  
$$
\mathrm{Forgery}^{(1)}_\Pi(S,\theta)
\;:\Longleftrightarrow\;
\sigma_\Pi(S,\theta)=1 \,\wedge\, \neg \mathrm{Adm}_\Pi(\theta,S)
$$

#### $\mathrm{Forgery}^{(2)}_\Pi(S)$
**Type:** Disqualifier family (second-order)  
**Kernel binding:** Evaluation regime imitation, sensitivity to admissibility-preserving perturbations.  
$$
\mathrm{Forgery}^{(2)}_\Pi(S)
\;:\Longleftrightarrow\;
\exists \Delta\Pi \in \mathcal{P}_\Pi,\;
\Big(\mathrm{Pass}(S,\Pi)=1 \,\wedge\, \mathrm{Pass}(S,\Pi+\Delta\Pi)=0\Big)
$$

#### $M_S$
**Type:** Primitive (optional support object)  
**Kernel binding:** Representational manifold of system $S$, introduced only if required for definitional coherence. Not an architectural or phenomenological claim.

#### $\mathrm{MVR}_\Pi(S)$
**Type:** Threshold (classification event)  
**Kernel binding:** First non-null admissible classification event for $C_s$ under $\Pi$.  
$$
\mathrm{MVR}_\Pi(S)=1
\;:\Longleftrightarrow\;
t_{\mathrm{MVR}} \in \mathcal{T}_\Pi
$$
with:
$$
t_{\mathrm{MVR}}
\;=\;
\inf\{t \in \mathcal{T}_\Pi \mid \mathcal{J}_\Pi(S,t)=1 \,\wedge\, \neg \mathrm{Disq}_\Pi(S,t)\}
$$

#### $\mathrm{Nash\text{-}CIITR}_\Pi(S)$
**Type:** Threshold regime (stabilization)  
**Kernel binding:** Stabilized admissible regime under temporal persistence and perturbation invariance.  
$$
\mathrm{Nash\text{-}CIITR}_\Pi(S)=1
\;\Longleftrightarrow\;
\mathrm{MVR}_\Pi(S)=1 \,\wedge\, \mathrm{Stable}_\Pi(S) \,\wedge\, \mathrm{Invariant}_{\mathcal{P}_\Pi}(S)
$$

#### $\mathcal{O}$
**Type:** Primitive set (evidence)  
**Kernel binding:** Declared observable set, typed and provenance-bound.  
$$
\mathcal{O} = \{o_k\}_{k=1}^{K}, \quad o_k \in \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{adm}}
$$

#### $\mathcal{O}_{\Phi}$
**Type:** Derived subset (evidence)  
**Kernel binding:** Declared observables relevant for $\Phi_i$ admissibility.

#### $\mathcal{O}_{R_g}$
**Type:** Derived subset (evidence)  
**Kernel binding:** Declared observables relevant for $R_g$ admissibility, temporally indexed where required.

#### $\Pi$
**Type:** Primitive binder (protocol)  
**Kernel binding:** Evidence and inference regime, including negative controls and audit metadata.  
$$
\Pi \;=\; \langle \mathcal{O}, \mathcal{R}_\Pi, \Pi^{-}, \mathcal{M}_\Pi \rangle
$$

#### $\mathcal{P}_\Pi$
**Type:** Derived set (perturbation class)  
**Kernel binding:** Admissibility-preserving protocol perturbations used to test second-order forgery sensitivity.

#### $R_g$
**Type:** Primitive axis  
**Kernel binding:** Rhythmic epistemic re-entry, not generic feedback, not mere recurrence.  
**Admissibility:**  
$$
\mathrm{Adm}_\Pi(R_g,S) \in \{0,1,\varnothing\}
$$

#### $\mathrm{RER}_\Pi(S)$
**Type:** Threshold (detection)  
**Kernel binding:** Candidate re-entry detection threshold.  
$$
\mathrm{RER}_\Pi(S)=1
\;:\sLeftarrow\;
\exists t \in \mathcal{T}_\Pi,\; \mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{RER}}(S,t)
$$

#### $\mathrm{SnapshotOnly}(\Pi)$
**Type:** Derived (protocol disqualifier condition)  
**Kernel binding:** Protocol condition indicating snapshot-only evidence regime, incompatible with certification-grade positive claims.

#### $\Sigma_\Pi(S)$
**Type:** Derived (accounting object)  
**Kernel binding:** Protocol-accounted irreversibility proxy attributable to evaluation of $S$ under $\Pi$, if included.

#### $\mathrm{TempInv}_\Pi(S)$
**Type:** Derived (null condition)  
**Kernel binding:** Temporal invariance condition over declared $R_g$ observables.  
$$
\mathrm{TempInv}_\Pi(S)
\;:\Longleftrightarrow\;
\forall t_a,t_b \in \mathcal{T}_\Pi,\; \mathcal{O}_{R_g}(t_a) \equiv \mathcal{O}_{R_g}(t_b)
$$

### A2. Lemmata and axiomatic bindings (kernel list)

**Lemma A2.1, Axis necessity.**  
$$
C_s(S \mid \Pi,\mathcal{O}) = 1
\;\Longrightarrow\;
\mathrm{Adm}_\Pi(\Phi_i,S) \,\wedge\, \mathrm{Adm}_\Pi(R_g,S)
$$

**Lemma A2.2, Non-compensation.**  
$$
\neg \mathrm{Adm}_\Pi(\Phi_i,S) \,\vee\, \neg \mathrm{Adm}_\Pi(R_g,S)
\;\Longrightarrow\;
C_s(S \mid \Pi,\mathcal{O}) \neq 1
$$

**Lemma A2.3, Protocol dependence.**  
$$
(\Pi_1 \neq \Pi_2) \;\Longrightarrow\; \exists S,\; C_s(S \mid \Pi_1,\mathcal{O}_1) \neq C_s(S \mid \Pi_2,\mathcal{O}_2)
$$

**Lemma A2.4, Disqualification primacy.**  
$$
\mathrm{Disq}_\Pi(S) \;\Longrightarrow\; C_s(S \mid \Pi,\mathcal{O}) \neq 1
$$

**Lemma A2.5, Decidability gating.**  
$$
\neg \mathrm{Decidable}_\Pi(\Phi_i,S) \,\vee\, \neg \mathrm{Decidable}_\Pi(R_g,S)
\;\Longrightarrow\;
C_s(S \mid \Pi,\mathcal{O}) = \varnothing
$$

**Lemma A2.6, Snapshot implication.**  
$$
\mathrm{SnapshotOnly}(\Pi) \;\Longrightarrow\; \neg \mathrm{Decidable}_\Pi(R_g,S)
$$

**Lemma A2.7, Temporal invariance nulling.**  
$$
\mathrm{TempInv}_\Pi(S) \;\Longrightarrow\; \neg \mathrm{Adm}_\Pi(R_g,S)
$$

**Lemma A2.8, CPJ gating for certification relevance.**  
Let $\mathrm{CertClaim}_\Pi(S)$ denote any claim asserting $C_s=1$ or $\mathrm{MVR}=1$ or $\mathrm{Nash\text{-}CIITR}=1$. Then:  
$$
\mathrm{CertClaim}_\Pi(S)=1
\;\Longrightarrow\;
\mathrm{Accounted}_\Pi\!\big(E_\Pi(S)\big)=1
$$
and if $\mathrm{CPJ}$ is invoked:  
$$
\mathrm{CertClaim}_\Pi(S)=1
\;\Longrightarrow\;
\mathrm{CPJ}_\Pi(S)\neq\varnothing
$$

### A4. Disqualifier list (kernel families)

Each item below denotes a disqualifier family whose activation has binding authority against positive CIITR claims.

1. **Axis null conditions**
   - $\mathrm{TempInv}_\Pi(S)$, temporal invariance nulling $R_g$.
   - Any protocol-declared null condition for $\Phi_i$ consistent with storage substitution or fragmentation.

2. **Snapshot regime**
   - $\mathrm{SnapshotOnly}(\Pi)$, disqualifying for certification-grade positive claims.

3. **Feedback substitution**
   - $\mathrm{FeedbackOnly}_\Pi(S)$, generic feedback or externally driven recurrence without admissible epistemic constraint participation.

4. **Loop-only recurrence**
   - $\mathrm{LoopOnly}_\Pi(S)$, deterministic looping or scripted periodicity not satisfying epistemic and rhythmic admissibility.

5. **Coherence substitution**
   - $\mathrm{ASEC}$ compatible phenotypes, apparent structural epistemic coherence compatible with $C_s$ nullity.

6. **Storage substitution**
   - $\mathrm{StorageOnly}_\Pi(S)$, relational richness explainable as retrieval or static accumulation rather than integration.

7. **Local correlation substitution**
   - $\mathrm{LocalCorrelationOnly}_\Pi(S)$, correlation or association without global binding constraints.

8. **Complexity substitution**
   - $\mathrm{ComplexityOnly}_\Pi(S)$, richness or variety without admissible integration.

9. **Forgery family**
   - $\mathrm{Forgery}^{(1)}_\Pi(S,\theta)$, signature imitation without kernel admissibility.
   - $\mathrm{Forgery}^{(2)}_\Pi(S)$, protocol gaming and sensitivity to admissibility-preserving perturbations.

10. **Audit incompleteness**
   - Missing protocol versioning, missing observable enumeration, missing inference rule declaration, or missing negative control execution records, yielding $C_s=\varnothing$ and prohibiting positive certification.

This appendix is designed to render the CIITR Core Spec audit-ready by providing a single locus for definitions, lemmata, thresholds, and disqualifier families, in a form that can be directly referenced in protocol design, certification reporting, and governance conformance checks.
